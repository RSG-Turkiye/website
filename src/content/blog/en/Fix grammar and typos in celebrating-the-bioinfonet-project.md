I met the ISCB RSG Türkiye team in 2011 at the first student symposium, which was held as a satellite meeting with HiBiT. I was working on a _de novo_ genome project, and it was my first time talking about a serious project as a graduate student at a symposium. Many of you can probably imagine my anxiety. But things got better immediately: I met colleagues from Turkey!

Some of you may have difficulty understanding why it would be “that” important and pleasing to meet colleagues. Well…

If you live and study in a developing country and are trying to study in a new research area, here’s your life’s new soundtrack: “All by Myself”. Every living thing needs a proper environment to become something. So, just as a plant needs soil, water, and light, becoming a scientist requires a scientific environment.

So, as a living organism, I made colleague-taxis and became a part of the group. Then we found out that we needed another taxis movement toward real scientific discussion. With this need, the BioInfoNet Project was born. We wrote a proposal to the ISCB (International Society for Computational Biology) Student Council. They kindly supported us, and here we are.

Today, as a student group, we have reached roughly 250 people around the world (and that number is increasing). We have completed two successful [webinars](https://www.bigmarker.com/communities/bioinfonet/conferences) given by top-notch researchers, in which we were really pleased to participate. We are planning a student symposium and many more webinars.

We will make new taxis movements whenever needed. Join us. [Communities are good](http://www.bigmarker.com/bioinfonet) :o)
